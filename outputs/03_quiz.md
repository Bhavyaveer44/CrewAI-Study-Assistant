Q1. What is the primary difference between a classical bit and a quantum bit (qubit)?
A. A classical bit can only exist in multiple states, while a qubit can exist in a single state.
B. A qubit can exist in multiple states simultaneously, while a classical bit can only exist in a single state.
C. A qubit is smaller in size, while a classical bit is larger.
D. A qubit is slower than a classical bit.
Correct answer: B. A qubit can exist in multiple states simultaneously, while a classical bit can only exist in a single state.
Explanation: A key feature of qubits is their ability to exist in multiple states at the same time, which is not possible with classical bits.

Q2. What is one of the main advantages of quantum computing?
A. It can only process a single calculation at a time.
B. It can perform many calculations at the same time, making it incredibly fast and efficient.
C. It can only solve simple problems, like basic arithmetic.
D. It can only process information stored in classical bits.
Correct answer: B. It can perform many calculations at the same time, making it incredibly fast and efficient.
Explanation: Quantum computing's ability to perform many calculations simultaneously is its primary advantage.

Q3. What is a significant application of quantum computing in the field of chemistry?
A. It can only predict the properties of simple molecules.
B. It can simulate complex systems like molecules, which can help us develop new materials and drugs.
C. It can only analyze the structure of a single molecule.
D. It can only test the chemical properties of a compound in a lab.
Correct answer: B. It can simulate complex systems like molecules, which can help us develop new materials and drugs.
Explanation: Quantum computers can simulate complex chemical systems, making it easier to develop new materials and drugs.

Q4. What is a challenge faced by quantum computing that affects online security?
A. Quantum computers are too slow to impact online security.
B. Quantum computers can only process information stored in classical bits.
C. Quantum computing raises concerns about the security of online transactions and the need for quantum-resistant encryption algorithms.
D. Quantum computers can only encrypt information, but not decrypt it.
Correct answer: C. Quantum computing raises concerns about the security of online transactions and the need for quantum-resistant encryption algorithms.
Explanation: Quantum computing poses significant challenges for online security due to the need for new encryption algorithms.

Q5. What is the term for the process of solving optimization problems using a quantum computer?
A. Quantum Encryption
B. Quantum Simulation
C. Quantum Machine Learning
D. Quantum Annealing
Correct answer: D. Quantum Annealing
Explanation: Quantum annealing is a computing technique that uses a quantum computer to quickly solve optimization problems.
